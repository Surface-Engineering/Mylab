About the Lab

Surface modification for high-performance materials and devices

The Surface Engineering Laboratory at Gyeongsang National University focuses on surface modification — designing functional surfaces, developing materials that control surface properties, and creating fine functional patterns through electrohydrodynamic jet printing for electronic devices.

Materials Synthesis & Characterization

Synthesis and characterization of materials for surface modification — including thin films, composites, and printing inks.

Surface Property Evaluation

Evaluating surface properties using tribometry and atomic force microscopy to understand friction, wear, and nanoscale behavior.

Surface Enhancement Methods

Developing new surface enhancement techniques such as texturing and modification to improve durability and function.

Electrohydrodynamic Jet Printing

Creating fine functional patterns via electrohydrodynamic (EHD) jet printing for high-resolution electronic devices.

Printed & Flexible Electronics

Microsupercapacitors, memristors, and transparent electrodes built with MXene and functional inks via EHD printing.

Energy Harvesting

Triboelectric and piezoelectric nanogenerators that harvest energy from vibration, wind, and raindrops.

Tribology & 2D Materials

Eco-friendly 2D-material lubricants and the nanotribology of graphene, MoS₂, and hexagonal boron nitride.
